## 🔐 The 3 Locks of Cyber Security

---

### 📖 Introduction

Have you ever wondered what cybersecurity experts are actually trying to protect?

It’s not just about stopping *hackers*.
At the heart of cybersecurity are **three important principles** known as the **CIA Triad**.

To make it simple, let’s use something everyone understands: **your banking app**.

---

### 🛡️ The CIA Triad

| Principle          | Meaning                      | Simple Example                        |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------- | ------------------------------------- |
| 🔒 Confidentiality | Keeping information secret   | Protecting your bank balance          |
| ✅ Integrity        | Keeping information accurate | Ensuring your balance is correct      |
| ⚡ Availability     | Keeping systems accessible   | Making sure the app works when needed |

---

### 🔒 1. Confidentiality — *The Secret*

Imagine checking your bank balance.

You want:

* **You** to see it ✅
* **Your bank** to see it ✅
* Random strangers ❌
* Nosy neighbors ❌

That is **Confidentiality**.

### 💻 In Tech Terms

Cybersecurity professionals use:

* Passwords
* Encryption
* Authentication systems

to make sure only authorized people can access sensitive information.

---

### ✅ 2. Integrity — *The Truth*

Let’s say you have **₦50,000** in your account.

You send **₦10,000** to a friend.

You expect your remaining balance to be:

```text
₦40,000
```

Not:

```text
₦0
```

And definitely not:

```text
₦100,000
```

That’s where **Integrity** comes in.

### 💻 In Tech Terms

Integrity means:

* Data has not been altered
* Information remains accurate
* Nobody tampered with the system

The goal is to keep data **truthful and reliable**.

---

### ⚡ 3. Availability — *The Access*

Picture this:

It’s Friday night 🍽️
You’re at a restaurant.
You need to pay.
But your banking app refuses to open.

Your money is still safe…

…but it’s useless because you **cannot access it**.

That is an **Availability** problem.

### 💻 In Tech Terms

Security teams work to ensure:

* Systems stay online
* Apps don’t crash
* Services remain accessible when users need them most

---

### 🎯 Conclusion

The next time you hear about a **security breach**, ask yourself:

* 🔒 Did someone steal a secret? *(Confidentiality)*
* ✅ Did someone change the truth? *(Integrity)*
* ⚡ Did someone shut systems down? *(Availability)*

Understanding these three principles is understanding the **heart of cybersecurity**.
