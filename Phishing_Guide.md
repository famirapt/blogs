## 🎣 Don’t Fall for the Trap: How to Spot a Fake Email

---

### 📖 Introduction

In Nigeria, we see them every day:

* “Congratulations! You’ve won a CBN Grant.”
* “You have been selected for an NNPC Job.”
* “Your bank account will be blocked immediately!”

These messages usually arrive through:

* 📧 Email
* 📱 SMS
* 💬 WhatsApp

They are called **Phishing Attacks**.

Their goal is simple:

> 🚨 Trick you into giving away your password, bank details, or money.

Here’s how to spot these scams like a pro.

---

### 🚩 Red Flag 1: Scary or Urgent Language

Scammers want you to panic.

You may see messages like:

```text id="f2s8dk"
Your account will be DELETED in 2 hours!
Click here NOW!
```
or

```text id="l0a5pw"
Immediate action required to avoid suspension.
```
---

### ⚠️ Why This Is Suspicious

Real banks and government agencies:

* Do not threaten customers
* Do not rush you with panic messages
* Usually give official notifications calmly

Scammers use fear to stop you from thinking clearly.

---

### ✅ What To Do

If a message sounds urgent:

* Pause
* Read carefully
* Verify through official channels

Never click links in panic.

---

### 📧 Red Flag 2: The Sender’s Address

Always inspect the **actual email address** — not just the display name.

---

### ✅ Real Example

```text id="s9k3nx"
info@cbn.gov.ng
```
---

### ❌ Fake Example

```text id="h7p4ez"
cbngrant-office247@gmail.com
```
---

### ⚠️ Why This Matters

Government offices and banks:

* Use official domains
* Rarely use free email services like Gmail or Yahoo

If the address looks strange, it probably is.

---

### 👤 Red Flag 3: Generic Greetings

Scammers send the same message to thousands or millions of people.

That’s why they often begin with:

```text id="u5m1cv"
Dear Customer
```
or

```text id="g2z9rl"
Dear Esteemed Winner
```

instead of your real name.

---

### ⚠️ Be Careful

Legitimate services usually know:

* Your name
* Your account information
* Your registered details

Generic greetings are a major warning sign.

---

### 🔗 Red Flag 4: Suspicious Links

Before clicking any link:

* 🖱️ Hover over it with your mouse
* 📱 Or long-press it on your phone

This reveals the real destination.

---

### ⚠️ Dangerous Example

A fake link may look like:

```text id="k6v2pt"
secure-bank-login-update.xyz/freebonus/84722
```

instead of the real bank website.

---

### ✅ Safe Practice

Only trust:

* Official domains
* Secure websites beginning with:

```text id="c4x8nf"
https://
```

When unsure:

* Open your banking app manually
* Or type the official website yourself

---

### 🛡️ Quick Anti-Phishing Checklist

| Check                     | Safe? |
| ------------------------- | ----- |
| 🚨 Uses panic or threats  | ❌     |
| 📧 Strange sender address | ❌     |
| 👤 Generic greeting       | ❌     |
| 🔗 Weird link destination | ❌     |

---

### 🎯 Final Thoughts

Phishing attacks work because they target:

* Fear
* Curiosity
* Urgency

The best defense is slowing down and checking carefully before clicking anything.

Remember:

> 🔐 A few seconds of caution can save your money and identity.
